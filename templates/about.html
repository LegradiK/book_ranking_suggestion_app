📚 About BookShelf

Built for readers, by a reader

The idea

What is BookShelf?

BookShelf is a personal book discovery tool built around one simple question: of the thousands of books in a genre, which ones are genuinely worth your time?

It combines reading-log popularity from Open Library with community ratings from Goodreads — giving each book a score that reflects both how widely read it is and how much people actually enjoyed it. No sponsored placements, no algorithmic noise. Just books.

Ratings

How ratings work

Each book is scored using two independent signals, then stored locally so browsing is instant.

📖

Reading activity

The total number of Open Library users who marked a book as want-to-read, currently reading, or already read. A proxy for real-world reach.

⭐

Goodreads rating

Community star ratings matched to each book by title and author. Reflects how much readers actually enjoyed it — scored out of 5.

✦ ✦ ✦
Data sources

Where the data comes from

BookShelf pulls from two public sources to build and score its catalogue.

Open Library

Book catalogue & reading logs

An open, community-maintained catalogue of millions of books. BookShelf uses their Search and Bookshelves APIs to discover titles and measure how widely read they are.

Goodreads

Community ratings

The world's largest book review community. Ratings are matched against the catalogue by title and author, with a direct Goodreads link saved for every entry. Where Goodreads data is unavailable, links may be sourced from Open Library.

Under the hood

How it works

1

Fetch from Open Library

Books are pulled from Open Library's Search API across nine genres, sorted by reading-log activity to surface the most relevant titles per category.

2

Count reading activity

For each book, we total everyone who marked it as want-to-read, currently reading, or already read via Open Library's Bookshelves API.

3

Match Goodreads ratings

Each book is searched on Goodreads by title and author. When a confident match is found, the star rating and a direct link are saved alongside it.

4

Stored & served instantly

Everything lives in a local SQLite database. No external calls at browse time — your shelf loads fast every time.
